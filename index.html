1. Introduction¶

The overall objective of this project is to analyze movies from the 1900s to 2017 to try and determine what factors have the most impact on a successful movie. In this sense, we defined "success" as how much money a particular movie grossed internatally. Then, by analyzing several key characteristics of each movie and comparing them to worldwide gross, we can look for correlations between these factors and success. Using this data, we determine the ideal characteristics a successful movie should have and also attempt to create a model that predicts the success of a movie.

The first step is to import all the different libraries we will need to use throughout this project.

3. Data Tidying¶

Looking at all the data that was available, we decided that we needed to narrow down our analysis to a few key factors. The factors we believed would be most interesting to analyze that could also have a significant impact on the success of a movie were time of release, genre, budget, runtime, and vote average. Each one of these factors will be explained more in depth in the follow sections.

Due to the amount of additional data, we proceeded to removing the following unncessary columns: adult, belongs to collection, homepage, overview, spoken languages, tagline, video, poster path, production companies, production countries, original language, original tite, vote count, id, imdb id, and status to create a tidier dataset that still met our needs. We are now left with the columns pertaining to our key factors as well as title of movie and revenue.

The next issue with our dataset is with the genre column. The genres here are listed in a string, formatted as if it were a map, containing an id and name for each specific genre. Since this format is very messy and would be hard to work with, we decided to instead assign each movie with an array of genres since each movie could fit into multiple categories. We went about doing this by using regular expressions to parse out each section of the string that contained the genre and adding it to the array.

Next, if the budget or revenue column contained "0's" or any non-number, we dropped the rows corresponding to them. We believe the zeros where there in place of missing data and keeping them would have skewed our data comparisons.

Finally, we decided to limit the movies to the first 500 based on worldwide gross. Limiting ourselves to the top 500 grossing movies not only makes the computations easier, but it also helps to make trends more apparent. We believe that since we are looking for characteristics of very successful movies, limiting ourselves to the 500 most successful would allow us to more accurately find defining characteristics as opposed to anaylzing all movies, regardless of success, from the 1900s to 2017.

Now that our data is tidy, we will now go about adding some information that will be utilized later on in our analysis.

The first column we add is "genre_value." Genre value corresponds to a numerical representation of the genres present in a particular movie. We calculate these values by first counting up the instances of each genre in the top 500 grossing movies. We then divide the count of each specific genre by the sum of all the counts of all the genres to obtain a ratio value corresponding to each genre as seen below.

The next column we add is "month_value." Similar to "genre_value", "month_value" represents a numerical representation for the release month of a movie. Unlike genre, however, month_value is obtained by binning together the following months: January and February, March and April, May and June, July and August, September and October, and November and December. The reason for doing so is when we calculated the number of movies that were released in a given month, we noticed that the number of occurrences for these binned together months were surprisingly similar. With these binned values, we then constructed the binned months ratio values, divided them by 500, and added them to our dataset based on the month of each movies' release date.

Conclusion¶

Based on the results of our analysis, we discovered several common trends among the top 500 movies. To reiterate, we found that having large budgets, large being defined as in excess of \$200,000,000, had the most positive impact on movie success. Then, having runtimes of over 135 minutes, genres of adventure, science fiction, and fantasy, and a voting average of over 7 had moderate effects on movie revenue. Then finally, we discovered that although most of the top 500 movies were released in May/June or November/December, there was no significant correlation between movie release date and movie success. However, these are far from the only factors that go into a successful movie which was evident when we tried to create a movie successfulness predictive model. This model produced mediocre predictions, resulting in skewed predicted data that could be explained by some other unexplored or unanalyzed variables. While we hoped to be able to create a more accurate regression model, the factors that define movie success proved to be more ambiguous than the surface level factors that most people, including ourselves, think about and could be an interesting area to revisit for further analysis in the future.
